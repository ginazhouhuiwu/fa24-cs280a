Automated Camera Trajectory Planning in 3D Scenes

Gina Wu (Fall 2024)

[Code]

Abstract

The ability to automatically generate camera trajectories in 3D scenes can revolutionize industries such as cinematography, game design, and robotics. This work proposes an end-to-end framework that takes a 3D scene representation as input and generates camera paths that are not only visually compelling, but also prioritize focusing on key objects and collision avoidance within the scene. The framework is composed of two core components: keyframe selection, which identifies critical viewpoints, and trajectory generation, which ensures smooth, collision-free transitions between keyframes. Ongoing and future work aims to extend this framework by incorporating more advanced techniques for scene understanding, adaptive cinematographic styles, and user-enabled trajectory adjustments to further enhance its versatility across use cases.
